Simple Interest: The Basics

Simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal amount. It’s straightforward and commonly used for short-term loans or basic savings accounts. The formula is:

Simple Interest = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100

For example, if you invest $1,000 at a 5% annual interest rate for 3 years, the interest earned is $150, and the total amount becomes $1,150. Use our simple interest calculator above to try it yourself!

Compound Interest: Growth Over Time

Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus any accumulated interest, leading to exponential growth. It’s common in long-term investments like mutual funds or savings accounts with compounding. The formula is:

Amount = Principal × (1 + Rate/Compounding Frequency)^(Compounding Frequency × Time)

Compound Interest = Amount - Principal

For instance, $1,000 invested at 5% annually, compounded yearly for 3 years, grows to approximately $1,157.63, with $157.63 in interest. Learn more about compound interest on Investopedia. Our compound interest calculator lets you adjust compounding frequency (e.g., monthly, quarterly) for precise results.
